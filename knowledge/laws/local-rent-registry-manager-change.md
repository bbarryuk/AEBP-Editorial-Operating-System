## Oakland — Rent Adjustment Program (RAP) Rental Unit Registration

**Confidence: Level 2 (local ordinance) for the requirement; Level 3 (agency guidance) for mechanics.** Source: Oakland's own Rental Unit Registration page, fetched directly 2026-07-18.

- Owners of units subject to the RAP fee must report rent and tenancy information to RAP **annually**, confirming or updating registered information no later than **March 1** each year. The 2026 renewal deadline is **March 2, 2026** (March 1 fell on a non-business day).
- Registration is done through the online **Rent Registry** at rentregistry.oaklandca.gov. Paper forms exist but the city explicitly warns of "significant delays" processing them — online is the recommended path.
- Owners who miss the deadline lose the ability to file petitions for rent increases, impose rent increases, or respond to tenant petitions, and failure to register becomes an affirmative defense in most eviction actions. This is a real, sourced consequence — cite it as such, not softened.
- **What the page does NOT confirm:** a specific "change of agent/manager" checkbox or an off-cycle update mechanism distinct from the annual renewal. The fetched page describes the annual cycle and a Property Registration Form (used mainly for claiming exemptions) but does not document a standalone mid-year "update your manager" workflow. **Do not claim a specific checkbox or off-cycle form exists for Oakland** unless a future fetch of the actual Rent Registration Forms page or the online portal confirms one. The safe, verified claim: contact RAP's Registration Unit directly (below) if a manager change happens between annual renewals, rather than asserting a specific self-service mechanism.
- Contact: rentregistry@oaklandca.gov. RAP fee questions (separate from registration) go to the Business Tax Office at (510) 238-3704 / BTWebSupport@oaklandca.gov — don't conflate the two; registration and the RAP fee are explicitly separate processes per the source page.

## Berkeley — Rent Stabilization Board Registration

**Confidence: Level 2 (local ordinance) for the requirement; Level 3 (agency guidance) for mechanics.** Source: Berkeley Rent Board's Registration page, fetched directly 2026-07-18.

- Most rental units in Berkeley must be registered with the Rent Board (fully covered and partially covered units both have registration obligations, though different substantive protections attach).
- Registration, annual fee payment, **and updating unit information** all happen through the online **Rent Registry** at rentregistry.cityofberkeley.info — the source page states this can be done "any time," not gated to an annual renewal window the way Oakland's is. This is the basis for the claim in post 8342 that Berkeley owners can update manager/contact info without waiting for a fixed annual cycle — a real distinction from Oakland worth preserving if either city's process changes.
- Registration Unit contact: rentregistry@berkeleyca.gov, (510) 981-7368 ext. 2, or an appointment via the Registration Unit's booking page. Address: 2000 Center Street, Suite 400, Berkeley, CA 94704.
- FY 2026-2027 registration fees were due July 2, 2026, with a partial/full penalty waiver available if paid by September 30, 2026 — a currency detail, not relevant to the manager-change claim, but worth knowing if this file is reused for a fees-focused post.

## Richmond — Rent Program Enrollment and Registration

**Confidence: Level 2 (local ordinance) for the requirement; Level 1-adjacent for the specific form, since the source page names it explicitly.** Source: City of Richmond's Enrollment and Registration page, fetched directly 2026-07-18.

- Richmond's Rent Board Regulations (Chapter 4, adopted Feb. 21, 2018) require landlords to enroll all rental units (except fully exempt ones) and register tenancies in controlled units.
- **This is the strongest, most specific of the three cities for a change-of-manager scenario.** The source page states directly: *"Need to Update your Contact information, Property Manager or the Status of your Rental Unit? Please complete the Amended Property Enrollment form."* This is a named, purpose-built form for exactly the scenario post 8342 describes — unlike Oakland (annual-cycle-only, confirmed) or Berkeley (portal update, confirmed but not a distinctly-named "change of manager" form).
- Filing options: email to rent@ci.richmond.ca.us, mail to 440 Civic Center Plaza, Suite 200, Richmond, CA 94804 (Attn: Registration and Billings Unit), or in person at the same address (2nd floor).
- Richmond also charges a Residential Rental Housing Fee and Business License Tax, paid via a separate Contractor/Landlord Login (password = highest APN of the owner's Richmond parcels, first nine digits, no dashes) — not directly relevant to the manager-change claim but worth knowing if Richmond content expands.

## Editorial note — why the three cities are described differently

The three write-ups above are deliberately not parallel in structure, because the underlying facts aren't parallel: Oakland's registration is annual-cycle-only as far as this file can verify (no confirmed off-cycle self-service update), Berkeley's is anytime via the portal, and Richmond has a distinctly named form for this exact scenario. Flattening these into one sentence ("update your Oakland/Berkeley/Richmond registration the same way") would overstate what's actually confirmed for Oakland. Any future post drafting from this file should preserve that distinction rather than smoothing it away for prose symmetry.
